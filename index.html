The Pistachios

Pistachios are the edible seeds of the Pistacia vera tree, which is native to Central Asia and the Middle East. They have a distinct, slightly sweet flavor and are often eaten as a snack, either raw or roasted, and are also used in various dishes, such as ice cream, baklava, and pistachio-crusted meats. Pistachios are known for their split shells, which occur naturally as the nut grows and are sometimes dyed red or green for visual appeal. These nuts are nutrient-dense, containing healthy fats, protein, fiber, and various vitamins and minerals.
